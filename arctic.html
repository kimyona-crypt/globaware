Save the Arctic

Imagine a world without the Arctic

For millennia people lived in the Arctic alongside some of the most elusive and majestic animals, and in some of the toughest conditions anywhere on Earth. But now all that is changing. The Arctic is heating up rapidly.

Why this Matters?

To have even a chance of saving the rapidly melting Arctic, we need to keep fossil fuels in the ground.

The problem

An oil spill in the Arctic would be a catastrophe. But corporations are intent on trying to drill there. We can't afford to drill for more oil if we want a hope of keeping temperature rise to below 1.5ºC.

What you can do

For millennia people lived in the Arctic alongside some of the most elusive and majestic animals, and in some of the toughest conditions anywhere on Earth. But now all that is changing. The Arctic is heating up rapidly.

1. See the state of the Arctic

The rapid changes our world is facing are hauntingly obvious in the Arctic.

The Arctic has warmed by over 3ºC, since the industrial age began. That’s twice as fast as the rest of the planet. Changes we see here are rippling across the world: sea levels are rising, and the climate systems we rely on are becoming more unstable and destructive.

2. OIL DRILLING

There is already more oil in the world than can be safely burnt. There is simply no sane argument for the huge risk of Arctic drilling. If governments won't act, it's up to people around the world to stand up to greedy oil companies and demand Arctic protection.

3. Learn about Threatened Arctic Wildlife

POLAR BEARS


When you think of Arctic wildlife, you peobably think of a polar bear. You won't find a larger predator anywhere else on land of Earth. As sea ice melts and drifts further apart, polar bears are being forced to swom further and further to hunt. No bear can swim forever.

Arctic Foxes



To survive the extreme cold, Arctic foxes build dens for themselves that burst with wildflowers in the white desert of the tundra. Experts at conserving energy, they are so light on their feet that they almost glide over the snow.

Narwhals


Narwhals are the unicorns of the sea. They prefer swimming in sea ice, to open water. Their incredible sense of hearing helps them navigate. But also means that whan the OIL industry look for new oil deposits by firing super-loud seismic blasts into the water, they risk killing any narwhals in the vicinity.

Puffins


Puffins are highly skilled fishers found on the many islands in the Arctic and North Atlantic. They often live in large groups with other species, thriving in some of the most biodiverse waters in the world.

Walruses


Walruses are known for their whiskers, tusks and sheer size. They are a keystone species in the Arctic ecosystem, which is why it is so worrying that we have seen thousands of them beaching themselves in recent years.
